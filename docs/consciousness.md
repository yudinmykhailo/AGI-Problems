# Consciousness and subjective experience

[← Back to list](../README.md)

**Category:** Philosophical  
**Problem status:** Open (no consensus)

---
## The essence of the problem

Even if all the technical problems in this repository are solved - one question will remain unanswered: will AGI truly understand, or will it only simulate understanding extremely convincingly?

This is not an academic question. The answer determines how we should treat AGI, whether we can trust its self-assessment of its values, and whether it carries moral status.

---
## Why it is critical

**The “Hard Problem of Consciousness”** (David Chalmers, 1995): There are “easy” problems of consciousness - explaining how the brain processes information, responds to stimuli, controls behavior. Neuroscience is gradually solving them. But the “hard” problem - why all this physical interaction gives rise to subjective experience, “what it’s like” to be, to see red, to feel pain - remains unexplained.

This means two things for AGI:

1. We cannot create a test that reliably distinguishes “real” consciousness from a convincing simulation - any behavioral test (including the Turing test) could theoretically be passed by a “zombie” - a system without internal experience.

2. We cannot reliably either confirm or rule out the presence of consciousness in a given system.

This has a practical consequence: aligning values through the system’s self-report is unreliable if we do not know whether it has “genuine” internal states that it describes.

---
## Additional context

**The “Chinese Room” thought experiment** (John Searle): A person in a room manipulates Chinese characters according to rules, without understanding their meaning, but produces correct answers. An argument against the sufficiency of syntactic information processing for understanding.

**Integrated Information Theory (IIT, Tononi)** : Consciousness is integrated information (Φ). Any system with high Φ has consciousness to some degree. Theoretically applicable to AI - and predicts that most current architectures have very low Φ despite high intelligence.

**Global Workspace Theory (GWT, Baars/Dennett)** : Consciousness is a “broadcasting” system that distributes information to many specialized modules. Architecturally closer to implementation in AI.

---
## How it is being attempted to solve

The honest answer: not yet - in terms of a solution. Theoretical work is ongoing.

**IIT (Tononi)** - a mathematical theory providing a quantitative measure of consciousness. Criticized for inapplicability to arbitrary substrates and counterintuitive predictions.

**GWT (Global Workspace Theory)** - a functional theory describing the architecture of conscious processing. More operationalizable.

**Higher-Order Theories** - consciousness as meta-representation: a system is aware of a state if it has a higher-order representation of that state.

**Neurobiological markers** - searching for correlates of consciousness in the brain (NCC) with the goal of later applying criteria to AI systems.
